# Industry-Specific Metrics for Stock Picking

## Quick Selection Guide

| Industry | Key Multiples | Key Operating Metrics | WACC Notes |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|------------|
| Technology / SaaS | EV/Rev, EV/EBITDA | ARR growth, NDR, Rule of 40, Gross Margin | Higher beta (1.1-1.5), low debt |
| Financials (Banks) | P/E, P/Book | ROE, ROA, NIM, Efficiency Ratio | Use DDM, not DCF |
| Consumer / Retail | EV/EBITDA, P/E | SSS, Inventory Turns, Gross Margin | Moderate beta, some leverage |
| Industrials / Manufacturing | EV/EBITDA, P/E | EBITDA Margin, Asset Turnover, CapEx/Rev | Cyclical — normalize |
| Healthcare / Pharma | EV/EBITDA, EV/Rev | R&D/Rev, Pipeline, Patent Cliff | High growth premia for biotech |
| Energy / O&G | EV/EBITDA, P/E | Reserve life, Production growth, Lifting cost | Commodity-sensitive, high CapEx |
| Utilities | EV/EBITDA, Div Yield | RAB growth, Regulatory ROE, Payout | Low beta, high leverage, stable CF |
| Real Estate / REITs | P/FFO, NAV | FFO growth, Occupancy, Cap Rate | Use DDM/NAV, not DCF |
| Telecom | EV/EBITDA, Div Yield | ARPU, Churn, CapEx/Rev, Subscriber growth | High CapEx, moderate leverage |
| E-commerce / Marketplaces | EV/Rev, EV/GMV | GMV, Take Rate, Active Users, AOV | Growth-stage may need EV/Rev |

## Detailed Industry Profiles

### Technology / Software / SaaS

**Must-have metrics:**
- Revenue Growth (YoY)
- Gross Margin (expect >65% SaaS, >50% hardware)
- Rule of 40 = Revenue Growth % + FCF Margin %
- Operating Margin trend (path to profitability)

**Add if available:**
- ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) and NRR (Net Revenue Retention)
- CAC Payback, LTV/CAC
- Gross Retention Rate
- RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations)

**Preferred multiples:** EV/Revenue (growth stage), EV/EBITDA (mature), EV/FCF
**Key driver for premium:** Rule of 40 score, NRR >110%, durable growth

**DCF considerations:**
- Projection period: 7-10 years (long runway for high-growth)
- CapEx is low (mostly R&D capitalization and cloud infra)
- Stock-based comp is material — always account for dilution
- Working capital changes often favorable (deferred revenue)

---

### Financial Services (Banks, Insurance, Asset Managers)

**Must-have metrics:**
- ROE (Return on Equity)
- ROA (Return on Assets)
- NIM (Net Interest Margin) — for banks
- Efficiency Ratio — for banks
- CET1 Ratio / Tier 1 Capital — for banks

**Add if available:**
- Loan growth, NPL ratio, Coverage ratio
- Combined Ratio — for insurance
- AUM and fee rates — for asset managers
- Cost of Risk

**Preferred multiples:** P/E, P/Book (vs. ROE regression)
**Key driver for premium:** ROE consistently > cost of equity

**Valuation notes:**
- Do NOT use EV-based multiples or DCF (FCFF) for banks
- Use DDM or Excess Return Model
- P/Book vs. ROE scatter plot is the primary valuation tool
- A bank with ROE > Ke should trade above book value

---

### Consumer / Retail

**Must-have metrics:**
- Revenue Growth (YoY)
- Same-Store Sales (SSS) growth — for physical retail
- Gross Margin
- EBITDA Margin
- Inventory Turnover (COGS / Avg Inventory)

**Add if available:**
- Revenue per sq ft / Revenue per store
- Customer Acquisition Cost
- Online vs. Offline mix (and growth)
- Average Order Value (AOV) — for e-commerce

**Preferred multiples:** EV/EBITDA (mature), EV/Revenue (high-growth e-commerce)
**Key driver for premium:** SSS momentum, gross margin expansion, omnichannel execution

**DCF considerations:**
- CapEx includes store openings/renovations — separate maintenance vs. growth
- Working capital seasonality is important (inventory builds before peak seasons)
- Lease obligations (IFRS 16 / ASC 842) affect EV and leverage ratios

---

### Industrials / Manufacturing

**Must-have metrics:**
- Revenue Growth
- EBITDA Margin
- Asset Turnover (Revenue / Total Assets)
- CapEx / Revenue
- Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)

**Add if available:**
- Book-to-Bill ratio, Backlog
- Capacity utilization
- Geographic revenue mix
- R&D spend (for tech-heavy industrials)

**Preferred multiples:** EV/EBITDA (primary), P/E
**Key driver for premium:** ROIC > WACC, order book visibility, margin expansion

**DCF considerations:**
- Cyclical companies: use mid-cycle margins, not peak or trough
- CapEx is significant — model explicitly with maintenance vs. expansion
- Working capital can swing with cycles (inventory, receivables)
- Consider normalizing earnings over a full cycle (3-5 years)

---

### Healthcare / Pharma / Biotech

**Must-have metrics:**
- Revenue Growth
- R&D / Revenue
- EBITDA Margin (for profitable pharma)
- Pipeline stage distribution (Phase I, II, III, approved)

**Add if available:**
- Patent expiration timeline (cliff risk)
- Regulatory approval probabilities
- Peak sales estimates for key drugs
- Number of Phase III assets

**Preferred multiples:** EV/EBITDA (mature pharma), EV/Revenue (growth biotech)
**Key driver for premium:** Pipeline depth, near-term catalysts (approvals), patent protection

**Valuation notes:**
- Pre-revenue biotech: use risk-adjusted NPV of pipeline, NOT multiples
- Mature pharma: standard DCF works but model patent cliffs explicitly
- R&D is an investment — don't penalize high R&D spend without context

---

### Energy / Oil & Gas

**Must-have metrics:**
- Production volume (boe/d) and growth
- Reserve life (years of production)
- Lifting cost / Finding cost per boe
- EBITDA Margin
- FCF Yield

**Add if available:**
- Reserve replacement ratio
- Proved Developed % of total reserves
- Netback per boe
- Breakeven oil price

**Preferred multiples:** EV/EBITDA, P/E, EV/Proved Reserves, EV/Production
**Key driver for premium:** Low-cost production, reserve quality, FCF generation

**DCF considerations:**
- Commodity price assumptions are the #1 sensitivity — use strip prices as base
- Depletion accounting — reserves are consumed, not perpetual
- Terminal value may not apply in traditional sense — use reserve life
- Include abandonment and decommissioning liabilities

---

### Utilities

**Must-have metrics:**
- RAB (Regulated Asset Base) and growth
- Regulatory allowed ROE
- Dividend Yield and Payout Ratio
- Net Debt / EBITDA (leverage)
- FFO / Net Debt

**Add if available:**
- Rate case outcomes / tariff review schedule
- Renewable energy mix / ESG score
- Customer growth (connections)
- Distribution loss rates

**Preferred multiples:** EV/EBITDA, EV/RAB, Dividend Yield
**Key driver for premium:** RAB growth trajectory, regulatory stability, clean energy transition

**Valuation notes:**
- Highly leveraged by design — leverage ratios higher than other sectors (3-5x Net Debt/EBITDA is normal)
- Predictable cash flows → lower WACC (lower beta, typically 0.5-0.8)
- DDM works well — stable dividends with visible growth
- Brazilian utilities: use WACC regulatório as reference point

---

### Real Estate / REITs

**Must-have metrics:**
- FFO (Funds from Operations) and FFO/share growth
- Occupancy Rate
- NOI Margin (Net Operating Income)
- Cap Rate (NOI / Property Value)
- NAV per share

**Add if available:**
- Weighted Average Lease Expiry (WALE)
- Rental reversion rates
- Same-property NOI growth
- Debt maturity profile

**Preferred multiples:** P/FFO, Price/NAV (premium or discount)
**Key driver for premium:** Portfolio quality, low vacancy, above-market rental growth

**Valuation notes:**
- Use DDM or NAV-based valuation — NOT traditional DCF
- FFO = Net Income + D&A - Gains on property sales
- AFFO (Adjusted FFO) = FFO - maintenance CapEx — better proxy for distributable cash
- NAV = sum of property values (NOI / cap rate) - net debt

## Brazilian Market Adjustments

### Key Differences for B3-Listed Stocks

| Factor | US Market | Brazil (B3) |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Risk-free rate | 10Y Treasury (~4.5%) | NTN-B real rate + IPCA (~6-7% nominal, ~5-6% real) |
| Equity risk premium | 4.5-5.5% | 5-7% + country risk 2-4% |
| Tax rate | 21% federal | 34% (IR 25% + CSLL 9%) |
| Typical beta | Sector-dependent | Add Brazil systematic risk |
| Mandatory dividends | None | Minimum 25% of adjusted net income (most companies) |
| Currency | USD | BRL — add FX volatility to analysis |
| Inflation accounting | N/A | Consider real vs. nominal returns |
| Interest on equity (JCP) | N/A | Tax-deductible "dividend" — adds ~2-3pp to effective yield |

### WACC for Brazilian Companies

```
Ke = NTN-B (real) + β × ERP_Brazil + Spread_adicional (se small cap)
   OR
Ke = US Rf + β × ERP_US + CRP (country risk) + Currency risk premium

WACC = (E/V) × Ke + (D/V) × Kd × (1 - 0.34)
```

Common approach: use **Ke = SELIC + Equity Premium** as simplified check (should be ~15-18% nominal for most BR stocks in current rate environment).
